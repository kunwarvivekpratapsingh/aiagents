Multimodal AI

Multimodal AI refers to systems that can process and generate information across multiple modalities — text, images, audio, video, code, and structured data. Combining modalities enables richer understanding and more capable AI applications.

Core Modalities:
- Text: Natural language; the primary modality for LLMs
- Images: Pixel data; processed by CNNs or Vision Transformers (ViT)
- Audio: Waveforms or spectrograms; speech, music, environmental sounds
- Video: Temporal sequences of images; includes motion and events
- Structured data: Tables, graphs, code, mathematical expressions

Vision-Language Models:
These models jointly represent images and text in a shared embedding space.

CLIP (Contrastive Language-Image Pre-Training, OpenAI 2021):
Trained on 400M image-text pairs from the web using contrastive learning. The image encoder (ViT or ResNet) and text encoder (Transformer) are trained so that matching pairs have high cosine similarity. Enables zero-shot image classification: compare image embedding to text descriptions of all classes.

ALIGN (Google, 2021):
Similar to CLIP but trained on 1.8B noisy image-text pairs, showing that scale can compensate for data quality.

GPT-4 Vision / GPT-4o:
Natively multimodal — accepts image and text inputs, produces text. Achieves near-human performance on visual question answering, chart interpretation, document understanding, and image captioning.

Claude 3 (Anthropic):
The Claude 3 family (Haiku, Sonnet, Opus) accepts images as input with text context. Capable of chart analysis, diagram interpretation, document reading, and reasoning about visual content.

Gemini (Google DeepMind):
Natively multimodal from pre-training on text, images, audio, and video. Gemini 1.5 Pro processes up to 1 hour of video or 30,000 lines of code in a single context.

Vision Transformers (ViT):
Apply the Transformer architecture directly to images by splitting images into fixed-size patches (e.g., 16×16 pixels) and treating each patch as a token. Achieves state-of-the-art vision performance when trained at scale (ViT-L/16, ViT-H/14).

LLaVA (Large Language and Vision Assistant):
Open-source multimodal LLM connecting a visual encoder (CLIP ViT) to an LLM (LLaMA/Mistral) via a projection layer. Trained on visual instruction data. Family includes LLaVA-1.5, LLaVA-NeXT.

Image Generation Models:
- DALL-E 3 (OpenAI): Text-to-image generation tightly integrated with ChatGPT; generates high-fidelity images from detailed text prompts
- Stable Diffusion: Open-source latent diffusion model; run locally or via API
- Midjourney: Proprietary; aesthetic focus; Discord interface
- Imagen (Google): Cascaded diffusion model; strong at photorealism

How Diffusion Models Work:
Diffusion models learn to reverse a gradual noising process. Forward: gradually add Gaussian noise over T steps until the image is pure noise. Backward: train a neural network (U-Net or Transformer) to predict and remove noise at each step. At inference: start from random noise, denoise step by step.

Latent Diffusion Models (LDM):
Operate in a compressed latent space (encoded by a VAE) rather than pixel space. Much more compute-efficient. Stable Diffusion is a latent diffusion model.

Speech and Audio:
- Whisper (OpenAI): Transformer-based automatic speech recognition (ASR); trained on 680K hours of audio; robust to accents and noise
- Bark, VALL-E: Text-to-speech with voice cloning
- MusicGen (Meta): Text-conditioned music generation
- AudioCraft: Family of audio generation models

Multimodal RAG:
Extending RAG to multiple modalities:
- Store image embeddings alongside text embeddings in the vector database
- At query time, retrieve relevant images and text chunks together
- Provide both visual and textual context to a multimodal LLM
Applications: Searching technical documentation with diagrams, medical imaging QA, product search with photos.

Challenges:
- Alignment between modalities: Ensuring the model correctly associates visual and linguistic concepts
- Training data: Paired multimodal data is scarcer and more expensive than text-only data
- Evaluation: Harder to evaluate generation quality across modalities
- Modality gap: Even with joint training, text and image embeddings often occupy different regions of the shared space
